Mona Lisa by Leonardo da Vinci

Location: The Louvre, Paris, France

Significance: Perhaps the most famous painting in the world, the Mona Lisa is renowned for its exquisite detail, the mysterious expression of the subject, and the mastery of techniques like sfumato. Its enduring popularity and cultural significance make it a symbol of the Renaissance and of art itself.



The Starry Night by Vincent van Gogh

Location: The Museum of Modern Art, New York, USA

Significance: Van Gogh's The Starry Night is one of the most recognized paintings in the world, celebrated for its vibrant colors, swirling patterns, and emotional intensity. It captures the beauty of the night sky with a sense of movement and energy that has captivated viewers for generations.



Spolarium by Juan Luna

Location: National Museum of Fine Arts, Manila

Significance: The Spoliarium is arguably the most famous painting in the Philippines. It won the gold medal at the Exposición Nacional de Bellas Artes in Madrid in 1884. The painting depicts fallen gladiators being dragged into a chamber, symbolizing the oppression of the Filipino people under Spanish colonial rule. It is a powerful statement on the struggle for freedom and has become a symbol of Filipino nationalism.
